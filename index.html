End Of Plastic Pollution

By Mustafa Imad (7th Grader)

Published : 3/8/2022 Word Count : 559

“At least 14 million tons of plastic end up in the ocean every year, and plastic makes up 80% of all marine debris found from surface waters to deep-sea sediments.” (Marine plastic pollution | IUCN) Plastic alternatives include sugar + carbon dioxide, Smart mud, Mushrooms, shrimp Shells, and Edible Water Bottles. Plastic alternatives are the best way to reduce plastic pollution because it biodegrades, sea life is impacted by plastic pollution, and recycling requires 500 years of plastic decomposition and more people to participate in the process.

Plastic alternatives biodegrade. Most plastic alternatives are made of natural substances. For example, smart mud and mushrooms are compostable, renewable, and edible. “Plastic alternatives is a renewable alternative to fossil-fuel-based polymers, potentially inexpensive, and, because it is biodegradable, will not contribute to growing ocean and landfill waste,” explained Antonine Buchard. Do you know what happens when it goes into the ocean?

Sealife is impacted by plastic pollution. Trillions of sea animals die every year. Some marine animals eat plastic and then die because they cannot digest it. Unlike traditional plastic, some plastic alternatives are edible. ¨ Thousands of seabirds, sea turtles, seals, and other marine mammals are killed each year after ingesting plastic or getting entangled in it. Endangered wildlife like Hawaiian monk seals and Pacific loggerhead sea turtles are among nearly 700 species that eat and get caught in plastic litter.” (de Bruijn and Johnson) Because most plastic alternatives are edible, using them instead of traditional plastics can save lives. How are plastic alternatives better than recycling plastic?

Recycling requires 500 years and more people to participate in the process. Most plastic alternatives are compostable so they can decompose on their own. On the other hand, plastic cannot decompose by itself so it needs to be recycled. “Approximately 8 billion tons of plastic have been produced since the 1950s and more than 300 million tons are now made each year. Although recycling has been mainstream for decades, and mechanical recycling works well for rigid plastics such as PET and mono materials, 10% of all plastic ever made has actually been recycled, at best” (recycling alone won't solve the plastic waste crisis). Although plastic is reusable, it still can not decompose. Evidence shows us how much recycling has solved the problem, but we need something else to solve the problem because 90% is still left. How can we solve the problem?

Plastic alternatives are the best way to solve the problem because they can be decomposed on their own, marine animals are dying from eating plastic, and to recycle one plastic takes 500 years to recycle, and also more people. Plastic alternatives are made of natural substances such as Smart mud and Mushrooms. Trillions of sea animals die every year because they cannot digest plastic. Plastic alternatives can decompose on their own. Everyone needs to take action to solve the problem before it becomes worse than ever.
